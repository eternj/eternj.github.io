How to Sideload Kodi to Apple TV Using atvloadly

Posted on 2026-02-15 Edited on 2026-02-23

Overview

The goal of this article is to share how I installed (sideloaded) Kodi to my Apple TV 4K (2nd Gen). Kodi is a media center and entertainment hub that I have been using since pre-Kodi era (when Kodi was XBMC). It has a beautiful interface and is easy to use. I mainly use Kodi to watch and record over-the-air (OTA) TV programs, as well as watch ripped blueray movies. Kodi has powerful add-ons that can expand and enhance its features and capabilities. It officially supports Windows, linux, and android. Unfortunately, Apple TV, which runs tvOS, does not have an official version of Kodi in App Store. This means we cannot download and install Kodi from App Store. Instead, we need to find some unofficial way to install or sideload it to Apple TVs.

atvloadly is a web service that supports sideloading apps on Apple TV. In this article, I’ll share how I setup an atvloadly docker and how I used atvloadly to sideload Kodi to my Apple TV. Hopefully this will help someone else who’s looking to do the same.

Workflow and Prerequisites

  1. Workflow

    • Setup an atvloadly docker.
    • Add an Apple account to atvloadly.
    • Pair the atvloadly web service with the Apple TV.
    • Sideload Kodi to the Apple TV.

  2. Prerequisites

    • A computer running linux. Unfortunately, this method does not work on Windows. It doesn’t need to be a full-fledged laptop or desktop. You can use a Raspberry Pi if you have one lying around. I used a single board computer called Rock64 running a stripped down version of Debian.
    • A burned Apple account. As noted on the atvloadly website, it is a good ideal to use a dedicated Apple ID or Apple account for sideloading. A free Apple account will work. I just created a new free Apple account for this purpose. You can create one on the Apple Developer Account webpage.
    • A phone for two-factor authorization (2FA). When creating a new Apple account for sideloading, you need a phone for 2FA. atvloadly needs to be authorized as a trusted device (it will be virtualized as a MacBook). When adding the Apple account to atvloadly, Apple will send a 2FA verification code to the registered phone number and you will need to enter the code and authorize it.

Assuming the host machine IP is 192.168.1.12, you can access the newly created atvloadly server by typing http://192.168.1.12:5533 in your web browser and hit enter. If everything goes right, you should be greeted by the atvloadly homepage.

Add Apple Account to atvloadly

First thing to do is to add your newly-created Apple account to atvloadly following the steps below:

  1. On the atvloadly homepage, select “PREFERENCES –> Accounts”.

  2. Click “ADD ACCOUNT” button.

  3. The “Login Apple Account” window opens, like this:

  4. Input the email and password of your newly-created Apple account and click “LOGIN” button.

  5. A second window will pop up and ask you to input a security code that was just sent to your phone. Enter that code and click “OK”.

  6. After that you should see your Apple account listed on the Accounts page.

Pair Apple TV

Now it’s time to pair your Apple TV in atvloadly. My Apple TV 4K (2nd Gen) does not have a usb port so I couldn’t use a USB cable to connect it to my PC. I had to pair it wirelessly. To do this, I followed the instructions below:

  1. On your Apple TV, open the Settings menu. Then select “Remote and Devices –> Remote App and Devices”. This will put your Apple TV in the pairing mode.
  2. Refresh the atvloadly homepage. Now you should be able to see your Apple TV with word “Pairable”.
  3. Click on your Apple TV to pair. Your Apple TV will display a pairing code on your TV. Enter that code in your atvloadly page. Now your Apple TV should be successfully paired in atvloadly.

Sideload Kodi to Your Apple TV

Once you successfully paired your Apple TV, you can start sideloading to your Apple TV using atvloadly. Follow the instructions below:

  1. Download the unsigned Kodi ipa file from here. Make sure you download the one for tvOS, not iOS.

  2. Open atvloadly home page on your computer. Click the Apple TV that you paired in the last step. A new window will pop up, like this:

  3. Click “CHOOSE FILE” button and select kodi-tvos_21.2.ipa you just downloaded.

  4. Select your Apple account from the dropdown list.

  5. Click “INSTALL” button and atvloadly will do its magic and install Kodi to your Apple TV. This step will take a while since Kodi is a decent size app so be paitent. Behind the scene, I believe atvloadly does the following: 1) creating a developer certificate from your Apple account, 2) signing the Kodi ipa using that certificate, and 3) installing Kodi to your Apple TV after signing.

  6. After atvloadly shows “Installation Succeeded!” in its message box, Kodi has been successfully installed to your Apple TV (as shown below). You can now go to your Apple TV and enjoy Kodi -:)

Additional Notes

  • As of this writing, the latest atvloadly version is v0.3.3. The latest tvOS is v26.1. They work together very well.
  • Since I used a free Apple account to sideload Kodi to my Apple TV the certificate is only good for 7 days, which means Kodi will stop working after 7 days. However, by default, atvloadly will automatically refresh (re-sign) the Kodi app on Day 6. That way, you can keep using Kodi without manually sideloading Kodi again after 7 days.
  • You can use a paid Apple developer account to sideload Kodi. In that case the installation is good for 365 days. However, since atvloadly automatically refresh the Kodi installation every 7 days, you don’t need to pay for a real Apple developer account to have a “permanent” working Kodi app on your Apple TV.
  • For Kodi, I only use two add-ons with it: NextPVR and Jellyfin add-ons. NextPVR add-on allows me to watch over-the-air (OTA) TV programs. Jellyfin add-on allows me to watch ripped TV series and blueray movies. Both add-ons work flawlessly for me. I haven’t tried other add-ons in Kodi.
